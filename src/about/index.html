What is this?

This experiment is part of a project done by us (Benedikt Klinglmayr, Samo Kolter, Mario Stoff, and Tobias Ungerb√∂ck), four Master's students in Informatics at TU Wien, as part of the User Research Methods course. In this project, we explore how and when people listen to music and other forms of auditory media (e.g. Podcasts, audio books) and how it influences them, both on an emotional level and in terms of performance, using a mixed-methods approach.

Our goal with this experiment is to find out whether background music influences participant performance in a selection of cognitive tests. Due to lack of time and financial resources, it was impossible for us to do this experiment on-site proved difficult. So, we instead built this web app that allows anyone to participate in our experiment from anywhere they want. We hope to collect as many responses as possible. If you haven't participated yet, we would be very grateful if you take the time for it as well!

Experiment Setup

Every website visitor is assigned to one of two test groups that both complete the same set of tasks. The only difference is that one group is asked to complete the tasks in complete silence, while the other should listen to instrumental music from this YouTube video in the background.

The task performance data is stored in the cloud automatically and will be collected and analyzed by us at the end of the experiment trial period (some time in January 2023). No personal data is stored in the process.
